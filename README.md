# Super-OSSM
Introducing the Super OSSM
A clean sheet, modular design, for a choose your OSSM build adventure.

Building on community improvements to the R+D design, the Super OSSM is compatible with 4 motor types, 2 PCBs, 3 rail & belt sizes, with 2 drive types to choose from, plus add-on modules.

My approach is to make it compact and compatible, only increasing the diameter by 15mm from the standard PitClamp ring, whilst being able to offer a wide range of compatibility for different-sized components. 

Large focus on - reducing the number of parts required, with the main unit being only 4 3D printed parts - simplifying the BOM, with standardised bolt sizes & lengths and using standard OSSM parts - ease of 3D printing, by minimising the need for support material - and improved assembly & serviceability,  with a twist-lock to access the motor and PCB. 
The final goal has been to improve the overall aesthetics so that the Super OSSM looks like a finished product rather than a DIY project.

Unfortunately, two design compromises were made - first, support and compatibility for the legacy iHSV motor was not possible, due to the backpack electronics that it has, making packaging inside the Super OSSM impossible - second, the use of 688 (8x16x8mm) bearings, rather than standard OSSM bearings, to aid ease of assembly.

Design features include a larger version of @Armpit’s PitClamp design with 120 position / 3° increments, and a fixed or folding clamp option for easy under-bed storage.

Support for @BuildingRandomStuff’s ALT PCB and the R+D PCB, with a translucent logo for the status LED.

An alternative drive option to use @SaladDressing’s Capstan drive system.

Support for -  
Motors	:	42AIM, 57AIM, 60AIM series & 60AIM40F  
PCB	:	R+D & ALT  
Rails	:	MGN12, MGN15 & HGR20  
Drive Mechanism	:	Belt or Capstan  
Belts	:	2MGT, 3MGT & HTD-3M  
Add-ons	:	Stroker Module 	(hopefully more in future)

Special thanks to @Frayd for their input and putting up with me being "extra" on calls to discuss the project.

I hope to have designed an OSSM for one and an OSSM for all.
